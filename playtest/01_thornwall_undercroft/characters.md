## Pre-Session Discussion (Character Creation)

> **Alex:** Okay, so we've got 18 points across 9 attributes, min 1 max 3. That's actually tight — everyone defaults to 2, and each point above or below is a trade. No dump stat below 1, no stat above 3. I like that there's a real ceiling.

> **Sam:** And no stat above 3 at creation. That +1 max modifier means even the best character is only marginally better than average at anything. The system really does flatten the power curve.

> **Riley:** The Spark system seems like it's meant to compensate for that. You can't be amazing at a thing, but you can choose moments to be amazing.

> **Alex:** Right. So for party balance — we need one of each Facet? Body, Mind, Soul?

> **Sam:** That seems logical. Body for combat and physical stuff, Mind for investigation and magic, Soul for social and magic. Whoever takes Mind or Soul can grab a magic domain.

> **Riley:** I'll take Soul. I want to play the social character, and Communion looks interesting — Sense the Unseen at Tier 1 plus a magic domain.

> **Sam:** Mind for me. I'll go Guild Apprentice for the magic-granting background. Inscription domain — focused type, so easier difficulties. Encoding spells into surfaces, laying traps, that kind of thing.

> **Alex:** Body it is. Classic front-liner. I'll go City Watch Veteran — Combat Practiced, Endurance as secondary skill. I want to be able to take hits and deal them.

## Player First Impressions of Character Creation

> **Alex:** Fast. Really fast. I'm used to D&D character creation taking an hour with all the class features and spell selection. This was maybe 15 minutes once I understood the system. The constraint of 18 points across 9 attributes with a 1-3 range means there aren't that many choices to agonize over, but the choices feel meaningful because the modifiers are so tight.

> **Sam:** I like that magic comes from Background, not class. It means I'm a scholar who also does magic, not "a wizard." The domain system is interesting — I picked what my magic does thematically, not from a spell list. But I'm not sure how creative I can get with "Inscription" in the moment. I'll need to see how the MM interprets intent.

> **Riley:** The lack of spell lists is both exciting and terrifying. "Domain + Intent + Scope" is very open-ended. I can see how that would be amazing with a good MM and frustrating with a bad one. The pre-technique limitation is smart though — it keeps us from going wild at level 0 while still letting us do small magic things.

> **Riley:** One concern: Persuade is my best social skill but it's still only +1 (Charisma modifier, no skill bonus yet). At Standard difficulty, I'm rolling 2d6+1, which gives me... what, about a 58% chance of partial success or better? That feels low for the "face" of the party.

> **Sam:** That's the PbtA heritage. 7-9 is a success — you get what you want, just with a cost. In D&D terms, you'd fail ~42% of Standard checks at level 1. Here, you fail ~42% too, but failure means the story moves forward, not "nothing happens." It's a different philosophy.

> **Alex:** The combat system looks elegant on paper. Simultaneous posture declaration, exchanges instead of initiative order, conditions instead of HP. I'm curious how it plays. My main question is whether fights feel tactical enough or if they're just "roll and narrate."
