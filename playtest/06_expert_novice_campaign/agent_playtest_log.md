# Playtest Log: The Great Crossroads (Agent-on-Agent Simulation)

This document records a simulated playtest of *Facets of Origin* performed by the four playtesting personas we created:
1.  **Cyrus (Expert MM)** — Narrative-first, rules-fluent, spotlight-focused.
2.  **Arthur (Novice MM)** — Rules-rigid, turn-based habit, easily overwhelmed.
3.  **Valerie (Expert Player)** — Tactical optimizer, active roleplayer, Spark calculator.
4.  **Billy (Novice Player)** — Rules-confused, passive, Spark-hoarder.

The playtest is split into two halves to compare how different personas handle the exact same ruleset under combat and magic conditions.

---

## Part 1: The Ambush at the Crossing (Run by Arthur - Novice MM)

**Arthur (Novice MM):** Okay, so... you are walking down the stone highway, and suddenly three bandits jump out of the bushes! They have clubs. Uh, let's roll initiative. Everyone roll a d20.
*Billy looks confused and starts searching his sheet.*

> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "Wait, Arthur. In *Facets of Origin*, there is no initiative or d20s. Combat is resolved in simultaneous exchanges using 2d6. We should declare our postures first."
>
> **Arthur (Novice MM):** "Oh! Right, right. Let me look that up... Page 45 of the Manual. Right. Combat exchanges. Okay, so what postures are you guys choosing?"
>
> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "I'll take the **Aggressive** posture. I draw my shortsword and lunges at the lead bandit. That gives me +1 to my attack roll, but my reaction cost will be +1. What about you, Billy?"
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "Uh... I don't know. What does Balanced do?"
>
> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "It's called **Measured** posture here. No bonuses, but no penalties. It's safe."
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "Okay, I'll do that. I want to cast a spell... a fireball? Can I cast a fireball?"
>
> **Arthur (Novice MM):** "Let me check the spell list... Wait, there are no spell lists in this book? How do I know how much damage a fireball does?"
>
> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "Billy has the **Resonance** domain from his Temple Acolyte background. It's freeform. He doesn't cast 'fireball'—he manipulates sound and vibrations. Billy, what are you trying to do to the bandits with sound?"
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "Oh. Can I make a really loud noise to make their ears bleed and stun them?"
>
> **Arthur (Novice MM):** "Loud noise... okay, that sounds like a **Minor** scope intent. But since Billy is pre-technique, it becomes one step harder. So standard difficulty becomes **Hard** difficulty (-1). Billy, roll 2d6 and add your Spirit modifier."
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "What's my Spirit modifier? My Spirit attribute is 2, so is it +0?"
>
> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "Yes, Billy. Spirit 2 is a +0 modifier. But since this is a Hard roll, you have a -1 penalty. You should spend a Spark to gain an extra die!"
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "No, I want to save my Sparks for later. I'll just roll."

### [Mechanical Resolution: Exchange 1]

* **Billy's Resonance Spell (Hard Difficulty, -1 modifier):**
  - Roll: 2d6 (3, 2) - 1 = **4** (Failure, 6-).
* **Valerie's Attack (Aggressive Posture, +1 modifier, Dex 3 + Combat Practiced = +2 modifier. Total +3 modifier):**
  - Roll: 2d6 (4, 4) + 3 = **11** (Full Success, 10+).

**Arthur (Novice MM):** Okay, so Valerie, your attack is a 11. That's a full success. You hit the lead bandit. Since he's a Mook (TR 1), your strike defeats him instantly. He drops his club and runs away.
But Billy, your roll is a 4. That's a failure. Um... nothing happens?

> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "Actually, Arthur. On a 6- failure, the MM should deploy a consequence or advance the danger. The bandits should strike back."
>
> **Arthur (Novice MM):** "Oh, right! The rule says 'Things Go Wrong.' Okay, so the second bandit swings his club at Billy. It deals a strike (2 damage). Billy, how do you react?"
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "Can I dodge? How much does it cost?"
>
> **Arthur (Novice MM):** "Dodge costs 1 Endurance. Do you have enough?"
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "I have 4 Endurance. So I have 3 left now. Do I roll?"
>
> **Arthur (Novice MM):** "Yes. Roll Dexterity."
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "Dexterity is 2, so +0. Roll..."
  - *Roll:* 2d6 (5, 4) + 0 = **9** (Partial Success, 7-9).
>
> **Arthur (Novice MM):** "A 9 is a partial success. You dodge, but... let's say you fall over into the mud. You are now prone."
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "Okay, I'm in the mud. Can I cast my spell now?"
>
> **Arthur (Novice MM):** "No, the exchange is over. We have to do another exchange."

## Part 2: The Storm on the Bridge (Run by Cyrus - Expert MM)

**Cyrus (Expert MM):** Let's switch the scene. You have moved past the crossing and are now navigating the high stone bridge of Oakhaven. The wind is howling, throwing freezing sleet into your faces. A massive **Wind Elemental (Named NPC, TR 7)** blocks the bridge. Its body is a swirling vortex of air and debris. 
Valerie, Billy, the wind is whistling through the stone arches. What postures are you choosing as the elemental rises?

> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "I'll choose the **Defensive** posture. The wind is strong, and I want to stay low, anchoring myself with my shield to protect Billy from being blown off the bridge. My reaction cost is discounted by 1."
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "I'll choose **Measured** posture. I want to try that sound spell again, but this time I want to spend a Spark! I want to vibrate the air around the elemental to disrupt its vortex."
>
> **Cyrus (Expert MM):** "Excellent choice, Billy. Using Resonance to disrupt a wind vortex is a brilliant use of your domain. Because you are targeting its physical structure, this is a **Minor** intent. Pre-technique bumps it to **Hard** difficulty (-1). But you are spending a Spark, so you roll 3d6 and keep the highest two. Add your Spirit modifier (+0) and subtract 1 for difficulty."
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "Okay! Rolling 3d6..."
  - *Roll:* 6, 5, 2 (keep 6 and 5) = 11 - 1 = **10** (Full Success!).
>
> **Billy (Novice Player):** "I got a 10!"
>
> **Cyrus (Expert MM):** "A full success! The high-frequency hum from your staff matches the rotational frequency of the vortex. A loud *CRACK* echoes, and the elemental's wind wall shatters. It is **Staggered** (takes a Tier 2 condition). Because it is staggered, all attacks against it this exchange become **Easy** (+1 difficulty modifier)."

> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "Now is my chance. Since the elemental is staggered and open, I lunge forward to strike its core with my mace. I'll spend 1 Endurance to **Press** for an extra die!"
>
> **Cyrus (Expert MM):** "Pressing is a great move here. You roll 3d6 (base + Press). Modifier is Strength 3 (+1) + Combat Practiced (+1) - 1 (Defensive Posture) + 1 (Easy target from Billy's spell) = +2 modifier."
>
> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "Rolling 3d6..."
  - *Roll:* 5, 5, 1 (keep 5 and 5) = 10 + 2 = **12** (Full Success!).
>
> **Cyrus (Expert MM):** "A 12! Your mace smashes into the elemental's central core. A standard strike deals 2 damage. The Wind Elemental has **No Armor** but has 7 Endurance. It takes a strike (2 damage), dropping its Endurance pool from 7 to **5**."
>
> **Cyrus (Expert MM):** "Simultaneously, the elemental lashes out. It shoots a blast of compressed air at Billy (deals a strike, 2 damage). Valerie, you are in Defensive posture. Do you want to intercept?"
>
> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "Yes! I'll use my **Intercept** reaction to shield Billy. Intercept normally costs 2 Endurance. In Defensive posture, it only costs **1 Endurance**."
>
> **Cyrus (Expert MM):** "Roll Strength + Combat to block the blast (Standard difficulty)."
>
> **Valerie (Expert Player):** "Strength + Combat is +2. Roll..."
  - *Roll:* 6, 3 = 9 + 2 = **11** (Full Success).
>
> **Cyrus (Expert MM):** "You raise your shield just in time. The blast of air slams into the iron banding, dispersing harmlessly. Billy is untouched, and Valerie takes no damage. The exchange ends. Because the elemental has a Tier 2 condition (Staggered), it remains off-balance, but its wind vortex begins to reform."

## Post-Simulation Evaluation

### Playtester Persona Comparison

1.  **Arthur (Novice MM) vs. Cyrus (Expert MM):**
    *   **Arthur** struggled with the narrative-first resolution. When Billy failed, Arthur's initial reaction was "nothing happens," which is an anti-pattern in PbtA-derived games. He also defaulted to traditional TTRPG initiative loops rather than simultaneous combat exchanges.
    *   **Cyrus** flowed seamlessly. He interpreted Billy's creative magic intent, set the difficulty dynamically based on scope/tier rules, and used simultaneous action declarations to resolve attack, magic, and defensive reactions (Valerie's Intercept) in a single, coherent narrative step.
2.  **Valerie (Expert Player) vs. Billy (Novice Player):**
    *   **Valerie** showed deep mechanical optimization. She used Defensive posture to reduce reaction costs, spent Endurance to Press when the odds were in her favor, and protected her squishy magical ally.
    *   **Billy** initially suffered from "hoarding paralysis" with his Sparks, causing him to fail a basic spell. Under Cyrus's guidance in Scene 2, he spent a Spark to roll 3d6, securing a critical success that staggered the boss.
