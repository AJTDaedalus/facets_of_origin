# Scenario: The Iron Crucible

## Purpose (PT03 — Boss Stress Test)

Test what happens when a Boss fight goes the distance — no lateral solution available. This is the most critical gap in our playtest data: Boss phase changes are a flagship mechanic with zero validation.

### What We're Testing

1. **Phase Change mechanics** — Do they create interesting mid-fight pivots or just more HP?
2. **Deadly encounter survivability** — Is it actually ~25% win rate or 0%?
3. **Spark spending under pressure** — Does desperation overcome the hoarding pattern?
4. **0-Endurance Absorb spiral** — How fast does the death cascade happen?
5. **Is losing this fight FUN?** — A Deadly encounter should feel epic even in defeat.

### Key Metrics to Track

- Win/loss outcome
- Number of exchanges
- Sparks spent (per player, and when during the fight)
- PCs Broken (which ones, what exchange)
- Phase Change moment — did it change tactics?
- Player statements about fun/frustration (direct quotes)

## MM's Prep Notes

### Campaign Pitch (One Paragraph)

The party has been hired to clear an abandoned forge-temple beneath the old mining district. Previous teams haven't returned. The local smithing guild says something ancient woke up down there — the Iron Crucible, a construct-guardian forged during the founding wars. The guild wants it deactivated. The catch: the temple's architecture is collapsing, and the only exit seals behind you. There is no running from this fight.

### Session Structure

**Act I — The Descent (10 min):**
Brief setup. The party enters the forge-temple. Environmental details: heat, old anvils fused to the floor, walls scored by ancient fire. The corridor narrows, a portcullis drops behind them. They hear grinding metal ahead. Pure setup — no rolls, no combat.

**Act II — The Iron Crucible (Full session):**
One fight. One room. The Boss attacks when they enter. No lateral solutions are available by design — we need to test the Boss mechanics straight. The MM should NOT offer creative shortcuts this session.

**Act III — Aftermath (5 min):**
Win or lose, brief debrief. If the party wins, the construct deactivates and they find the exit mechanism. If they lose, describe the aftermath cinematically — the Crucible standing over their Broken bodies, returning to dormancy. Either way, the guild sends a recovery team.

### Environment

**The Forge Chamber:**
- Large circular room, 40 feet across
- Central forge pit (dormant, but radiates heat — narrative flavor only)
- Four pillars supporting the ceiling (can be used for cover narrative, but no mechanical effect — we're testing raw Boss combat)
- Single entrance, portcullis sealed behind party
- Dim light from embedded ward-stones in the walls

### Why No Lateral Solutions?

Previous playtests (PT01, PT02) saw players bypass the climactic fight through clever lateral thinking. That's great game design — but it means we have zero data on what happens when a Boss fight plays out to completion. This playtest deliberately removes that option so we can validate the math.

## The Boss: The Iron Crucible

**Tier:** Boss
**Endurance:** 10
**Attack Modifier:** +2 (Offense value: 4)
**Defense Modifier:** +1
**Armor:** Heavy (Tier 3 → Tier 2)
**Techniques:** Forge Slam (area attack: targets two PCs in same exchange)
**TR:** Offense 4 + Durability 5 + Armor 2 + Technique 2 = **13**

**Personality:** Not sentient. Mechanical. Activates when intruders enter, attacks methodically. No dialogue, no negotiation.

**Posture:** Aggressive (incoming attacks are Hard to react to; +1 offense = total attack mod +3)

### Phase Change

**Trigger:** When the Crucible first takes a Tier 2 condition (Staggered or Cornered).

**What Changes:**
- Its armor cracks — Heavy → Light (Tier 2 → Tier 1 only)
- It becomes faster — attack modifier increases to +3 (was +2)
- Posture shifts to Measured (reactions no longer harder)
- New ability: **Heat Surge** — once per exchange, one random PC must make a Constitution check (Standard) or take the Winded condition

**Design Intent:** The phase change trades durability for danger. The Crucible becomes easier to hurt but more threatening. This should create a mid-fight pivot where the party's strategy must adapt.

### Encounter Budget Check

- PS 3 party: Deadly budget = 3 × 4 = 12
- Crucible TR 13 (solo, ×0.75 action economy) = effective TR ~9.75
- With tier-weighted formula: 13 × 1.25 (Boss weight) × 1.0 (solo group) = 16.25
- **This is above Deadly budget** — intentionally. We want to see if it's survivable.

### Variant: Reduced Boss (if first run is 0% win)

If the PS 3 party gets wiped in exchange 1-2 with no meaningful decisions, dial back:
- Endurance 8 (instead of 10)
- Attack Modifier +1 (instead of +2)
- TR recalculates to ~10
- Run again and compare

### Post-Fight Questions

1. How many exchanges did the fight last?
2. Did the phase change create a meaningful tactical shift?
3. When did the first PC get Broken? Was there anything they could have done differently?
4. Did players spend Sparks? When? Did they regret not spending them earlier?
5. Was the 0-Endurance Absorb spiral a death sentence or a tense but manageable state?
6. **Most important:** Was it fun? Even if they lost, did it feel epic?
