# Playtest Batch Report: Adventures 1 to 10

This report compiles the statistical results, risk evaluations, and rules feedback from all 10 live and agentic playtests conducted on the *Facets of Origin* tabletop engine. 

---

## 1. High-Level Statistics

Across the 10 playtests (1 initial session + 9 batch sessions), a total of **51 rolls** were resolved live on the server.

### Outcome Distribution
| Outcome | Roll Range | Count | Percentage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Full Success** | 10+ | 9 | 17.6% |
| **Partial Success (Success with Cost)** | 7-9 | 25 | 49.0% |
| **Failure (Things Go Wrong)** | 6- | 17 | 33.3% |
| **Total Rolls** | — | **51** | **100%** |

### Key Statistical Takeaways:
* **The \"Partial Success\" Engine:** Nearly half of all rolls resulted in a partial success (49.0%). This validates that the core engine succeeds in driving the narrative through compromise, trade-offs, and escalating complications rather than simple binary passes or blocks.
* **Lethality Check:** Out of the 10 playtests, **2 ended in total party wipeouts** (Playtest 4: *The Molten Abyss* and Playtest 10: *The Clockwork Workshop*). Both featured TR 9 boss fights combined with environmental hazards.

---

## 2. Risk Level Analysis

### 🔴 Aggressive Posture Risk: HIGH
* **The Mechanic:** Going Aggressive gives a +1 modifier to strikes but inflicts a +1 Endurance cost penalty on reactions.
* **Friction Point:** For starting characters (who only have 3-5 Endurance and no armor), this penalty is extremely punishing. In Playtests 1, 2, and 8, players declaring Aggressive postures failed their subsequent Dodge rolls because their modified cost exceeded their current pool, forcing them into raw, undefended hits.
* **Recommendation:** Under the current scaling, starting characters should only go Aggressive if they are shielded by a guardian in Defensive posture (as Alaric did for Pippa in Playtest 1).

### 🔴 Pre-Technique Magic Risk: VERY HIGH
* **The Mechanic:** Casting magic before unlocking a specific Facet Technique imposes a -1 difficulty modifier.
* **Friction Point:** The failure rate for magic casting across the sessions was **50%** (5 out of 10 casts failed). The pre-technique penalty makes casting a spell highly volatile, often leading to backfires or complete failures (such as Volt's roll of 2 in Playtest 9).
* **Recommendation:** GMs must emphasize that starting mages should spend Sparks to secure advantage dice (3d6) on crucial casts, or risk constant magic backfires.

### 🔴 Environmental Hazard Risk: CRITICAL
* **The Mechanic:** Hazards (magma flows, steam vents, icy slopes) impose conditions or restrict movement during combat exchanges.
* **Friction Point:** In Playtest 4 (*Molten Abyss*) and Playtest 10 (*Clockwork Workshop*), the combination of a high Threat Rating (TR 9) boss and active environmental hazards resulted in immediate TPKs (Total Party Kills). The players were overwhelmed by the action economy, unable to clear conditions while also parrying boss strikes.
* **Recommendation:** Boss fights at TR 9 should feature passive or highly telegraphed hazards rather than active, round-by-round threats, unless players are higher level (Facet Level 2+).

---

## 3. Gameplay Feedback & Rules Revisions

### For Game Designers:
1. **Reaction Cost Mitigation:** Introduce a passive rule where characters with Light or Heavy armor ignore the +1 reaction cost penalty of the Aggressive posture once per combat.
2. **First-Level Spell Packages:** To ease \"Spell List Anxiety\" for novice players (like Billy and Toby), the player's manual should include 3 pre-defined \"spell forms\" per domain to act as guidelines before players attempt full freeform magic.

### For GMs (Mirror Masters):
1. **Telegraphing TR 9 Bosses:** A TR 9 boss should never surprise a starting party. The environment must warn players of the boss's weaknesses or offer lateral escape routes (e.g. collapsing steam pipes) to bypass direct combat.
2. **Failing Forward on Magic:** Emphasize that magic failures (6-) should shift the environment (e.g. steam vents block a door) rather than just dealing raw damage to the caster.
