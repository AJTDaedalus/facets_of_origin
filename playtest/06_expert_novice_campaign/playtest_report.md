# Playtest Report: Mixed Experience Playtest 06

* **Session Date:** July 9, 2026
* **Location:** The Sunken Spire of Aethelgard
* **Evaluator:** Cyrus (Mirror Master)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This playtest was structured to analyze how players of different experience levels (2 experts, 2 novices) adapt to the core systems of *Facets of Origin*. The campaign session tested rule assimilation, the simultaneous exchange combat loop, freeform magic (Domain + Intent + Scope) without pre-defined spell lists, and the Spark resource economy.

The session successfully ran to completion in roughly 2 hours, showing that the core systems are easily taught but have significant tactical depth. 

---

## 2. Playtester Feedback & Observations

### Expert MM (Cyrus)
* **Pacing & Flow:** The lack of standard turn-based initiative allowed scenes to move fluidly. Resolving combat in simultaneous exchanges cut the time of typical skirmish encounters by half compared to traditional TTRPGs.
* **Mixed-Group Management:** I found it easy to run. The experts naturally stepped in to coach the novices on character creation and attribute allocation. However, explaining "pre-technique" magic difficulty modifications was the biggest cognitive bottleneck of the session.

### Expert Player (Luke - Alaric)
* **Posture System:** *"The defensive posture is brilliant. It makes playing a guardian actually feel active. By declaring Defensive, my reactions were discounted, allowing me to intercept a hit on Pippa for just 1 Endurance instead of 2. It lets martials play strategically rather than just rolling 'to hit' every round."*
* **Spotlight:** *"Because combat resolves simultaneously, nobody was left waiting 15 minutes for their turn. Even the novices stayed engaged throughout the entire exchange."*

### Expert Player (Sophia - Sylvia)
* **Magic Resolution:** *"Pre-technique magic feels hard but balanced. Without specialized techniques, casting a minor spell at Standard or Hard difficulty forces you to respect the risk. You cannot spam magic. You have to wait for the right moment and back it up with a Spark."*

### Novice Player (Toby - Thorne)
* **Spark Economy:** *"I was nervous about playing a magic-user without a spell list. When Cyrus told me I could use sound waves to jam the valves, it felt incredibly rewarding. The Spark economy is a great safety net. I knew my roll had a negative modifier (-1 for Hard), but spending a Spark to roll 3d6 kept me from failing."*
* **Cognitive Load:** *"Calculating the pre-technique modifier (-1 step harder) took a couple of explanations, but once I wrote the final adjusted difficulty modifier on my sheet, it was fine."*

### Novice Player (Penny - Pippa)
* **Combat Rhythm:** *"Simultaneous rounds are awesome! I loved that I didn't have to wait for my turn. I declared I was jumping into the water and attacking, and it just happened. It felt like an action movie."*
* **complication Frustration:** *"Losing my shortsword on a 7-9 partial success Athletics check to climb out of the water felt a bit harsh. I got out of the water but lost my primary weapon. Maybe there should be other options for complications, like getting fatigued or temporarily dropping it rather than losing it completely."*

## 3. Mechanical Analysis & Evaluations

### The Spark Economy
* **Status:** **Validated.**
* **Findings:** Starting with 3 Sparks is the correct baseline for a short session. Players spent them at key moments: Sylvia to secure a path trap, Thorne to jam the steam vents, and Pippa to land the critical strike on the Golem. The choice to spend *before* rolling creates healthy tension and rewards players who understand the math of their modifiers.

### Posture System & Reactions
* **Status:** **Validated.**
* **Findings:** The posture mechanics performed exceptionally well. Pippa's choice to go Aggressive allowed her to take out a Sentinel instantly but penalized her Dodge, forcing her into the cold water. Alaric's choice of Defensive posture allowed him to block attacks and intercept blows for cheap. This creates clear role-based synergy without forcing characters into static class boxes.

### Freeform Magic (Domain + Intent + Scope)
* **Status:** **Concept Validated; Usability Needs Work.**
* **Findings:** The open-ended nature of the magic system allows for fantastic lateral solutions (e.g., using Resonance to jam valves). However, novices struggle to conceptualize what they are allowed to do. They tend to ask "Can I do X?" rather than declaring their intent.

---

## 4. Recommendations for Rules Revisions

1. **Add a "Common Intents Reference Guide":**
   For each magic domain, the Rulebook should include a table of 3–5 common minor and significant intents. This will give novice players immediate inspiration during play (e.g., for Resonance: "Vibrate Lock," "Disrupt Weapon," "Soothe Mind").
2. **Refine Non-Combat Movement Complications:**
   Add guidelines for MM complications on physical movement checks. Instead of permanently losing essential equipment like weapons on a 7-9 roll, suggest temporary setbacks, such as:
   - *Fatigued:* Temporarily reduce maximum Endurance by 1 until rested.
   - *Dropped:* The item lands nearby and requires an action (or exchange declaration) to retrieve.
   - *Delayed:* You succeed, but you are separated from the group or lose time.
