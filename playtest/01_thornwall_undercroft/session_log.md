## ACT I — THE HOOK

### Scene 1: Alderman Brost's Office

**Jamie (MM):** *The Thornwall Municipal Archive smells exactly like you'd expect — old paper, dust, and that faint chemical tang of preservation oil. You're shown up a narrow staircase to the third floor, past rows of clerks bent over ledgers, to a door marked "A. BROST — SENIOR ALDERMAN." The office is meticulously organized. Every surface is clean except for one corner of the desk where a cup of tea has gone cold, untouched. Alderman Brost sits behind the desk with his hands folded precisely in front of him. He does not stand when you enter.*

**Riley (Mira):** Before anyone says anything, I want to read the room. Not as a roll — I'm just... observing him. The cold tea. The way he's sitting. Is he nervous or is he in control?

**Jamie:** Good instinct. You notice: his hands are folded perfectly, but his left thumb is pressing hard enough into his right palm that the knuckles are white. The tea being cold suggests he's been sitting here waiting for you — not working, just waiting. He's anxious but projecting calm.

**Riley:** *Mira gives a slight nod to the others, the kind that says "he needs us more than he's letting on."*

**Alex (Kael):** Kael steps forward first. "Alderman Brost. My old captain mentioned your name — said you were reliable. We're here about the job."

**Jamie (as Brost):** "Yes. Thank you for your discretion in coming. Please, sit." *He gestures to three chairs that have clearly been arranged ahead of time.* "The Archive has a... situation. In the lower stacks. The restricted section, sealed since before my tenure. Sixty years, perhaps longer."

**Sam (Vessa):** "What kind of situation?"

**Jamie (as Brost):** "Sounds. Movement. Three weeks ago, one of my clerks — Lira — went to retrieve a misfiled document from the upper stacks near the sealed door. She came back... unsettled. Said she heard something moving on the other side. Purposeful, she said. Not rats."

**Alex:** "And you didn't call the Watch?"

**Jamie (as Brost):** *He unfolds his hands for the first time, then folds them again.* "The Watch would close the entire building for investigation. The Archive serves the city's legal and commercial records. A closure would be... disruptive. Expensive. I need this handled quietly."

**Riley:** I want to roll Insight to figure out if he's telling the whole truth. He's holding something back — the way he's choosing words so carefully.

**Jamie:** Good call. That's Wisdom + Insight. You're Novice in Insight, so no skill bonus. Standard difficulty — he's being careful but not actively deceiving you.

> **[Roll #1] 2d6(6,1) + Wisdom(0) + Insight(0) + Standard(0) = 7 → Partial Success**

**Jamie:** Seven. Partial success. You get something, but not everything. Here's what Mira picks up: Brost is genuinely worried about the sounds, that's real. But he's *also* too prepared. The three chairs, the rehearsed briefing, the specific request for discretion. This wasn't a sudden decision. He's been thinking about this — and about you specifically — for a while. You don't know *why* he chose you or what he's not saying, but you know there's more.

**Riley:** "Alderman. You seem like a man who prepares carefully. You didn't just find three strangers off the street. Why us?"

**Jamie (as Brost):** *A pause. The first genuine one.* "Your Watch friend, Kael — Captain Dren — mentioned you were... capable and unlikely to ask questions that aren't relevant to the job." *He meets Riley's eye.* "I hope that assessment was accurate."

**Alex:** Kael grunts. "Dren's usually right. What's the pay?"

**Jamie (as Brost):** "Fifty silver. And a favor from the Archive — a research request fulfilled, no questions asked, at a future date of your choosing."

**Sam:** Vessa's eyes light up. "The research favor. That's worth more than the silver."

**Jamie (as Brost):** "I thought you might feel that way." *He slides a corroded iron key across the desk.* "This opens the sealed door. Or it did, sixty years ago. The lock may need... persuasion."

**Alex:** "We'll handle it. Anything else we should know?"

**Jamie (as Brost):** "The lower stacks were sealed for a reason. I don't know what that reason was — the records of the sealing are, ironically, among the documents stored down there. Be careful. And be discreet."

*Riley notes: Brost's Insight check revealed he's been planning this. The party takes the job.*

### Scene 1.5: Talking to Lira

**Sam:** Before we go down, can we talk to the clerk? Lira?

**Jamie:** Absolutely. She's at a desk in the upper stacks, organizing repair requests. Young — maybe nineteen. She keeps touching the back of her neck.

**Sam:** "Lira? Alderman Brost said you might be able to tell us what you heard."

**Jamie (as Lira):** *She looks up and immediately looks nervous.* "You're the ones going down?" *She glances toward the stairwell.* "It wasn't an animal. I know what rats sound like. This was... deliberate. Like something walking on stone, but with no rhythm to it. And then it stopped. Right on the other side of the door. Like it knew I was there." *She touches her neck again.* "And the cold. I felt cold on my neck, like... like breath. But there was nothing there."

**Riley:** "You're safe, Lira. Whatever it was, it's behind a sealed door." Mira puts a hand on her shoulder briefly. "Thank you."

**Sam:** I want to roll Lore to see if I know anything about archive constructs — magical guardians that were common in old institutional buildings.

**Jamie:** Knowledge + Lore, and you're Practiced in Lore so +1 bonus. I'll call this Standard — it's not obscure knowledge for a Guild-trained scholar.

> **[Roll #2] 2d6(3,2) + Knowledge(+1) + Lore(+1) + Standard(0) = 7 → Partial Success**

**Jamie:** Seven, partial success again. Vessa knows this: old institutions — archives, vaults, guild halls — commonly used ward-constructs for security. Animated guardians powered by inscribed ward-lanterns, usually following simple behavioral loops ("guard this area," "challenge unauthorized entry"). They're durable but not intelligent. The partial success means you know the general concept but not the specifics of *this* archive's defenses. You can't tell from up here what type, how many, or how to deactivate them.

**Sam:** "Constructs. Old ward-magic. They're probably what she heard — a guardian on patrol. The cold could be ambient ward-energy bleeding through the door."

**Alex:** "So we're fighting magical statues. Lovely."

**Sam:** "Not necessarily fighting. Constructs follow rules. If we can figure out the rules, we can work around them."

## ACT II — RISING ACTION

**Alex:** Can I force it? Just jam the key in and turn hard?

**Jamie:** You can try. That's Strength + Athletics (forcing a mechanism). Standard difficulty — the lock was maintained but the key is fragile. But I'll warn you: if you fail, the key might break.

**Jamie:** Three. Oh no. Snake eyes.

**Alex:** ...

The worn third glyph reveals itself not to her eyes but to her fingers, the vibration pattern different from the surrounding stone. She can feel the shape of it. She traces the sequence: first glyph, second, third. The lock mechanism clunks, groans, and the heavy door swings inward on hinges that were definitely oiled recently. Cold air rushes out. Below, stone steps descend into darkness.

**Riley:** *Mira takes a breath.* "After you, Kael." *(Sparks remaining: 2)*

**Sam:** "That was beautiful. Also, did anyone else notice the hinges were oiled? Someone has definitely been down here."

### Scene 5: Mook Ambush (Combat — Skirmish)

**Alex:** That's me. I step out and strike the nearest one.

**Jamie:** Okay. This is the first combat of the session. Let me walk through the mechanics.

*(Jamie checks MM5 Quick Reference)*

**Jamie:** Dust Constructs are Mooks. No Endurance, no condition track. One successful Strike at any tier removes them. They don't react — they apply pressure through numbers. You're hitting first, before we even declare postures. Roll Strike: Strength + Combat skill, Standard difficulty.

> **Kael [Roll #11]: 2d6(3,2) + Strength(+1) + Combat(+1) + Standard(0) = 7 → Partial Success**

The other two snap their featureless faces toward him.*

**Alex:** "Two left. Come on!"

**Jamie:** Now we enter a proper exchange. Everyone declares posture simultaneously. Remember: Aggressive (+1 offense, +1 reaction cost), Measured (baseline), Defensive (-1 offense, -1 reaction cost), Withdrawn (no offense, free reactions, recover 2 End).
