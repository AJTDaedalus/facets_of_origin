# Scenario: The Sunken Spire of Aethelgard

## MM's Prep Notes (Cyrus - Expert MM)

> I'm designing this playtest specifically to evaluate how a mixed group (2 expert players, 2 novice players) handles the core systems of *Facets of Origin*. I want to test:
> 1. Narrative-first exploration under pressure (Act I).
> 2. Skirmish-scale combat using simultaneous exchanges and posture management (Act II).
> 3. Freeform magic adjudication and lateral puzzle solving (Act III).
> I'll use the structured encounter budget to ensure the combat remains challenging but survivable.

### Campaign Pitch
The Lake of Sorrows has receded, exposing the upper levels of the Sunken Spire of Aethelgard—a flooded academy of arcane science that collapsed during the Great Fracture a century ago. Deep within its core lies the **Hearth-Core**, a dormant artifact of thermal magic that once protected the surrounding valley from freezing winters. The local village council of Oakhaven has pooled their savings (80 silver pieces) to hire the party. Their mission: infiltrate the spire, bypass its ancient automated security, retrieve the Hearth-Core, and return it before the onset of the winter frosts.

---

### Session Structure

#### Act I: The Wet Gate (20 minutes)
* **The Scene:** The party arrives at the exposed spire by boat. The entrance is a half-submerged stone archway blocked by a massive, rusted bronze gate.
* **Challenges:**
  - **Social/Lore:** Novices and experts examine the gateway. The gate is locked by an early-era Weaver glyph lock.
  - **Mechanical/Lateral:** The gateway can be forced open physically (Strength + Athletics), deciphered magically (Investigate/Lore + Inscription/Resonance), or bypassed by swimming underneath through a collapsed structural block (Dexterity + Athletics, risking cold exposure).

#### Act II: The Flooded Scriptorium (50 minutes)
* **The Scene:** The party stands in a vast, circular chamber filled with floating rotten parchment and chest-deep water. Submerged paths offer narrow walkways, but movement off them requires swimming.
* **The Ambush:** Four **Water-Logged Sentinels (Mooks)** rise from the deep water.
* **Combat Focus:** Introduce the simultaneous exchange system. Test how novices adapt to choosing postures (Aggressive, Balanced, Defensive, Withdrawn) and using reactions (Dodge/Parry) while dealing with water terrain hazards (which makes moving off paths Hard difficulty).

#### Act III: The Hearth-Core Vault (50 minutes)
* **The Scene:** A dry, steam-filled vault at the center of the spire. The Hearth-Core sits in a central pedestal, guarded by the **Sentry Golem (Named NPC)**.
* **The Boss Fight:**
  - **The Sentry Golem (TR 9):** An ancient stone and bronze guardian. It attacks with crushing heavy swings and steam vents.
  - **Phase Change:** When the Golem is first **Staggered** (takes 3+ damage in a single exchange), it begins venting superheated steam. All close-combat rolls become Hard difficulty unless players find a way to cool it down (e.g., redirecting the chamber's water pipes or using Resonance magic to disrupt the valves).
  - **Ending:** Once the Golem is defeated or deactivated, the party retrieves the Hearth-Core. The spire begins to rumble and flood rapidly—a cliffhanger rush to escape.

### NPCs & Key Figures

* **Alderman Vance (Social):** The desperate leader of Oakhaven. He represents the village's fear of the coming winter. Eager to pay but honest about the danger.
* **The Sentry Golem (Named NPC):** A mindless, loyal automaton programmed to protect the core. It speaks in clicking brass tones: *"Intruders detected. Authorization required. Core containment protocols active."*

---

### Enemies

#### Water-Logged Sentinel (Mook) — TR 1
* **Attack Modifier:** +0 (Offense value: 2)
* **Endurance:** None (Mook: one Strike removes it)
* **Armor:** None (0)
* **Description:** Skeletons of the spire's former guardians, fused with rotting organic material and animated by residual water magic. They carry rusted spears and lunging claws.
* **TR Calculation:** Offense 2 + Durability 0 = 2. As Mooks, scaled down to TR 1.

#### The Sentry Golem (Named NPC - Boss) — TR 9
* **Tier:** Named NPC
* **Endurance:** 8 (Durability value: 4)
* **Attack Modifier:** +1 (Offense value: 3)
* **Defense Modifier:** +0
* **Armor:** Medium (Armor bonus: 2)
* **Technique (Steam Blast):** Once per exchange, can target all attackers in close range with a blast of blinding steam (Technique bonus: 1).
* **TR Calculation:** 4 (Durability) + 3 (Offense) + 2 (Armor) + 1 (Technique) = **TR 9**
* **Lateral Solutions:**
  - **Pipes:** Redirection of freezing lake water from the broken overhead pipes to cool the golem (Athletics or Craft to break the valves).
  - **Inscription:** Writing a disruption glyph on its chassis to lock its joints (Lore + magic).
  - **Resonance:** Creating a counter-vibration to crack the golem's stone joints (Attune + magic).

### Encounter Budget Check

* **Party Strength:** 4 characters (each has 1 career advance = Party Strength 4).
* **Scene 2 (Flooded Scriptorium):**
  - 4 Water-Logged Sentinels × TR 1 = TR 4.
  - Multiplier: 4 enemies (×1.0 modifier).
  - Effective TR: 4.
  - Budget: Skirmish (4 × 1 = 4). Matches exactly. Highly survivable but introduces combat mechanics.
* **Scene 3 (Hearth-Core Vault):**
  - 1 Sentry Golem = TR 9.
  - Multiplier: Solo enemy (×0.75 modifier).
  - Effective TR: 6.75 (rounded to 7).
  - Budget: Standard/Hard boundary (4 × 2 = 8 max). Fits comfortably. Encourages players to use lateral puzzle elements to mitigate the golem's medium armor and steam blast technique.
