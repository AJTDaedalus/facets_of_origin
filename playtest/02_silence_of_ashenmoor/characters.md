## Player First Impressions of Character Creation

> **Taylor:** Fast. Fifteen minutes. The digital tool's Builder tab walked me through it — select Facet, distribute 18 points across attributes (the tool counts them for you and shows remaining), pick Background, done. No class features to compare, no feat trees to optimize. The constraint is the 1-3 attribute range with 18 total points. I miss the depth of D&D character creation, but I don't miss the hour it takes.

> **Casey:** The Builder tab is excellent for this. It shows you the backgrounds available for your Facet, with skill details. I could see immediately that Guild Apprentice gives me Lore Practiced and Investigate Novice with a mark. The domain selection was simple — pick one from your Facet's list, read the one-line description, choose. No spell list to agonize over.

> **Morgan:** I was worried I wouldn't understand the system well enough to make good choices. But the tool's Rules Summary in the Tools tab had everything I needed. When I wasn't sure what Attune did, I checked the skill description right there. When I couldn't remember the magic difficulty table, it was one click away. The tool is genuinely functioning as training wheels.

> **Drew:** Taylor walked me through it, but honestly the Builder did most of the work. I put points in attributes, the tool showed me modifiers. I picked Mercenary Guard because the description sounded cool, the tool filled in my skills. I selected a weapon, it calculated my modifier. I was done in ten minutes. I've spent longer creating characters in video games.

> **Drew:** One thing I don't understand yet: Sparks. I know they add dice, and you spend them before rolling. But when do I earn them? Taylor says "when you do something cool" but that's vague. I'll figure it out during play, I guess.
