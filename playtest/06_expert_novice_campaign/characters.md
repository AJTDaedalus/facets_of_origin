## Pre-Session Character Creation Discussion

> **Toby:** Okay, so I want to play a spiritual healer or a priest, but the magic system doesn't have a "Cleric" class. How do I make that?
>
> **Sophia:** In this system, you don't pick a class; you select a primary Facet and a Background that grants your magic. If you want spiritual magic, choose **Soul** as your primary Facet and **Temple Acolyte** as your Background. That gives you the **Resonance** domain.
>
> **Toby:** Resonance? Like sound waves?
>
> **Cyrus (MM):** Exactly. Sound, harmony, vibrations. You can use it to sense spiritual echoes, soothe someone's mind with harmonious hums, or disrupt structures with high-pitch notes. It’s freeform.
>
> **Penny:** I want to be stealthy and agile—a rogue type! I see "Body" is a facet. Should I put all my points there?
>
> **Luke:** You have 18 points total to divide among 9 attributes. The minimum is 1 and the maximum is 3 at character creation. To be a rogue, you want high **Dexterity** (for Finesse and Stealth) and **Intelligence** (for trap-disarming and streetwise knowledge). I'd go with Dexterity 3, Intelligence 3, and maybe Luck 3 if you want to rely on critical rolls.
>
> **Penny:** Cool! So I'll go: Strength 2, Dexterity 3, Constitution 2, Intelligence 3, Wisdom 2, Knowledge 1, Spirit 1, Luck 3, Charisma 1.
>
> **Luke:** Perfect. That gives you a Body rating of 7 (Str 2 + Dex 3 + Con 2), Mind of 7, and Soul of 5. That's a solid, well-rounded profile.
>
> **Toby:** For my acolyte, I'll go Soul primary. Spirit 3 and Charisma 3. My dumps will be Strength 1 and Knowledge 1. I'll take **Resonance** as my domain. I see "Pre-Technique" makes everything one step harder. What does that mean?
>
> **Sophia:** Since we are starting at level 0 (1 career advance), we haven't unlocked specialized magic Techniques yet. For a Standard domain like Resonance, a Minor spell is normally Standard difficulty (+0). Because you're pre-technique, it becomes Hard (-1). It means casting magic will be risky, so you should rely on your **Sparks** to boost your rolls.
>
> **Penny:** Sparks? How do those work?
>
> **Luke:** You start with 3 Sparks. Before you roll, you can spend a Spark to roll 3d6 instead of 2d6, keeping the two highest dice. If a check is really important or you have a negative modifier, spend a Spark to shift the odds in your favor.
