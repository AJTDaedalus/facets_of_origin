# Scenario: The Silence of Ashenmoor

## MM's Prep Notes (Jordan)

> Folk horror, not splatter. The dread comes from wrongness — things that should make sound but don't, people who recognize you but can't speak, a village where even the river seems to flow quietly. The players should feel the creep before they understand the threat. Session Zero established lines around body horror and loss of agency — so the "silencing" effect is temporary and reversible, and I'll frame it as oppressive rather than violating.

### Campaign Pitch (One Paragraph)
The mining village of Ashenmoor has gone quiet. Not metaphorically — literally. Letters from the village stopped three weeks ago. A merchant who passed through reported that the residents moved normally but could not speak, could not sing, could not scream. The party is hired by a regional magistrate who needs answers before she sends soldiers. The job: travel to Ashenmoor, determine the cause, and resolve it if possible. Payment: 80 silver and a writ of authority.

### Session Structure

**Act I — The Approach (20 min):**
The party arrives at Ashenmoor at dusk. Environmental storytelling: no birdsong, no insects, the wind makes less noise than it should. They meet Alderman Hale, who communicates by writing on a slate. He's terrified but functional. Maren, an afflicted young woman, draws circles compulsively. Old Calder, the deaf former mine foreman, is the only person who can still speak — he can't hear himself being drained. Social scene: gather information, establish the threat, get the mine maps from Calder.

**Act II — The Mine (60 min):**
- **Scene 1: The Upper Tunnels** — Exploration. Old mining infrastructure, collapsed sections, environmental clues. The silence deepens as they descend — players notice their own footsteps getting quieter. Investigation checks to find the path.
- **Scene 2: The Husk Ambush** — 4 Husks (reanimated miners) emerge from side tunnels. Silent, grasping, trying to hold the party still. Standard encounter to introduce combat and establish the threat pattern.
- **Scene 3: The Hollow's Domain** — Deeper tunnels where sound dies completely. Light behaves strangely. They encounter The Hollow — a figure made of compressed silence. Named NPC encounter (Hard difficulty). The Hollow doesn't fight conventionally — it reaches for faces and pulls at voices.

**Act III — The Heart (40 min):**
- **Scene 4: The Resonance Chamber** — The mine opens into a natural cavern. At the center: a crystalline formation pulsing with absorbed voices. This is The Resonance — the source. Boss encounter (Deadly difficulty). Phase change when Staggered: releases a wave of stolen voices that forces Mind saves. Lateral solutions available.
- **Scene 5: The Aftermath** — If the Resonance is destroyed, voices return. The village slowly recovers. Maren speaks first — her circles were maps of the crystal's structure. Cliffhanger: the crystal was not natural. Someone put it there. The mine was sealed because Hale's grandfather knew.

### NPCs

**Alderman Hale** (Social)
- **Want:** Save his village, specifically Maren (his granddaughter)
- **Secret:** His grandfather sealed the mine. The family journal describes the crystal as "the gift that was planted." Someone — or something — placed it there deliberately.
- **Detail:** Communicates by writing on a slate with steady hands. The steadiness is deliberate; his hands shake when he puts the chalk down.
- **Hook for party:** Will give Calder's mine maps only after the party demonstrates competence (a social challenge).

**Maren** (Non-combat NPC, afflicted)
- **Detail:** Young woman, early 20s. Eyes track everything. Draws circles within circles on any surface — walls, dirt, her own skin with a fingernail. The circles are a cross-section of the crystal's resonance pattern. She understands what's happening but cannot communicate it.
- **Info (if someone with Attune or Resonance magic reads her drawings):** The circles are a frequency map. The crystal has a counter-frequency — a harmonic that would shatter it.

**Old Calder** (Social/Utility)
- **Detail:** Deaf since a mining accident 20 years ago. Can speak (loudly, as deaf people do). Has no idea the village is silent — he thinks everyone is "being rude." His deafness makes him immune to the Resonance's drain.
- **Info:** Has the mine maps. Knows the lower tunnels connect to a natural cavern "where the walls sing." Has never been to the cavern — it was sealed before his time.
- **Utility:** If brought into the Boss fight, his deafness makes him immune to Voice Weaponization and the phase change mind save. He can't fight, but he can act as a distraction or carry equipment.

### Enemies

**Husk (Mook)** — TR 1
- Former miners. Bodies intact, eyes white, move silently. Attack by grappling — they try to hold targets still.
- Attack modifier: +0 (Offense value: 2)
- No Endurance (Mook: one Strike removes)
- No armor
- Description: They don't bleed when cut. They don't react to pain. They simply grasp and hold until destroyed or until their target stops struggling. The horror is in the recognition — some of them are wearing name badges.
- TR: Offense 2 + Durability 0 = 2. Mook minimum TR 1. **Using TR 1.**

**The Hollow (Named NPC)** — TR 9
- A figure made of compressed absence. Where it stands, sound dies in a visible radius — you can see the border where your voice stops carrying. Humanoid silhouette, no features, moves like a shadow cast by something that isn't there.
- Tier: Named NPC
- Endurance: 7 (Durability value: 4)
- Attack modifier: +1 (Offense value: 3)
- Defense modifier: +0
- Armor: None (0)
- Technique 1: **Silence Aura** — Anyone who fails a reaction against the Hollow loses their voice until end of exchange. Cannot cast magic requiring verbal intent, cannot use Persuade/Perform. (Technique bonus: 1)
- Technique 2: **Phase Shift** — Partially incorporeal. All Strikes against the Hollow are Hard difficulty unless the attacker uses magical or silver weapons. (Technique bonus: 1)
- TR: 3 + 4 + 0 + 2 = **9**
- Personality: Not malevolent. A construct of the Resonance's overflow — excess silence given form. Destroying it doesn't anger anything. It simply reforms from the nearest pool of silence, eventually. This is a symptom, not the disease.
- Lateral Solutions:
  - Resonance magic can disrupt its form (Magic Strike, Standard difficulty)
  - Warding magic can contain it temporarily (blocks its Silence Aura)
  - Characters with Perform or Attune can "sing through" the silence field, maintaining their voice
  - Physical attacks with silver or enchanted weapons bypass Phase Shift

**The Resonance (Boss)** — TR 14
- A crystalline formation the size of a person, growing from the cavern floor. It pulses with light that follows no rhythm you recognize — then you realize the rhythm is words. Thousands of stolen voices, compressed into light. It is not alive. It is a device, and it is doing exactly what it was designed to do.
- Tier: Boss
- Endurance: 10 (Durability value: 5)
- Attack modifier: +2 (Offense value: 4)
- Defense modifier: +1
- Armor: Light — crystalline surface deflects blows (Armor bonus: 1)
- Technique 1: **Voice Weaponization** — Uses stolen voices as a psychic attack. Target rolls Soul saving throw instead of a normal reaction. Failure = Shaken (Tier 1). (Technique bonus: 1)
- Technique 2: **Cacophony** — Once per fight, forces ALL characters in the chamber to make Soul saves (Hard). Failure = Shaken + lose 1 Endurance from the psychic pressure. (Technique bonus: 1)
- Phase Change: When first Staggered, releases a wave of stored memories. Every character hears their own voice speaking their worst fear aloud. Each character makes a Mind save (Hard). Failure = Cornered (frozen by the weight of their own past). The Resonance's Staggered condition clears — it has shed the damage as a weapon.
- TR: 4 + 5 + 1 + 2 + 2 (phase change) = **14**
- Personality: None. It is a device. It does not want. It processes.
- Lateral Solutions:
  - Maren's circles are a frequency map — a character with Resonance magic or Perform can use them to find the counter-frequency (Attune or Perform + the drawings = shatter the crystal at Hard difficulty, bypassing the fight entirely)
  - Old Calder is immune to Voice Weaponization and the phase change (his deafness)
  - The crystal has physical weak points at the joints where it meets the cavern floor — a Maneuver (Athletics or Finesse) to target them makes the next Strike Easy
  - Warding magic can create a sound barrier, protecting the party from Cacophony
  - The mine supports overhead can be collapsed onto the crystal — environmental Maneuver (Athletics, Hard), dealing the equivalent of a Tier 2 condition

### Encounter Budget Check

**Party Strength:** 4 (each character has 1 career advance)

**Scene 2 — Husk Ambush:**
- 4 Husks × TR 1 = TR 4
- 4 enemies: ×1.1 action economy multiplier (Mook-only, 4-6 range)
- Effective TR: 4.4
- Budget: Standard (4 × 2 = 8). Below Standard budget.
- Intended as introductory — establishes the horror, tests basic combat. ~90% win rate.
- **Complication:** 2 more Husks emerge from collapsed tunnel in Exchange 2 = total 6. New effective TR: 6 × 1.1 = 6.6. Still below Standard but creates pressure.

**Scene 3 — The Hollow:**
- 1 Hollow (TR 9) + 2 Husks (TR 1 each) = raw TR 11
- 3 enemies: ×1.0 action economy multiplier
- Effective TR: 11
- Budget: Hard (4 × 3 = 12). Just below Hard. ~55% win rate.
- With Phase Shift making Strikes Hard unless magic/silver is used, effective difficulty is higher. Real win rate closer to ~45%.
- This is the first encounter that should genuinely threaten the party.

**Scene 4 — The Resonance:**
- 1 Resonance (TR 14), solo: ×0.75 action economy multiplier
- Effective TR: 10.5
- Budget: Hard (12). Below Hard budget in raw numbers.
- But: Phase change spawns the fight's second phase. After Stagger-clear + mind saves, add 2 Husks emerging from the walls = raw TR 16, 3 enemies ×1.0 = effective TR 16 = Deadly budget (4 × 4 = 16).
- Pre-phase: ~60% win rate (manageable). Post-phase: ~30% win rate (Deadly).
- The lateral solutions (counter-frequency, mine collapse, Calder's immunity) are designed to bring the effective difficulty back down. A party that investigates wins. A party that charges in gets badly hurt.

### Environmental Notes

**The Silence Gradient:**
As the party descends into the mine, sound progressively dampens. This is atmospheric but also mechanical:
- Upper tunnels: Normal sound. All mechanics work normally.
- Middle tunnels: Sound muffled. Verbal-only magic and Persuade/Perform rolls at +0 (no difficulty change, but the fiction notes the unease).
- Lower tunnels / Hollow's domain: Sound severely dampened. Verbal intent magic requires a beat of extra concentration — no mechanical penalty, but the MM should describe the effort.
- Resonance chamber: The Resonance's Voice Weaponization and Cacophony only work here. Outside the chamber, these abilities have no effect.

**Lighting:**
The mine has no active lighting. The party must bring their own. The Resonance's crystal provides an eerie pulsing light in the final chamber — enough to see by, but the shadows move wrong.
