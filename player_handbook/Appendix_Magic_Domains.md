# Appendix: Magic Domain Catalog

This appendix contains the full descriptions of all 21 magic domains available in Facets of Origin. For the system rules governing how domains work — scope, intent, difficulty, rolling, and Sparks — see **Chapter II.3 (Magic)**.

> **Reading the domain descriptions:** Each domain lists effects that are *beyond its focus*. These are not hard walls — they are signals for where a domain's strength ends and another's begins. If a player attempts something borderline, the MM's default should lean toward yes, possibly at increased difficulty or with the suggestion that a different domain would handle it more naturally.

> **Reading the example intents:** Each domain lists three example intents per scope — Minor, Significant, and Major. These are **design patterns, not a menu**. They exist to calibrate what kind of ambition each scope licenses, not to enumerate a fixed list of "spells" a player is limited to. A player is always free to invent an intent the examples don't cover, so long as it stays within the domain's stated boundaries. If an example ever gets treated at the table as the one correct way to use a domain at a given scope, that is the appendix failing at its one job — flag it.

---

## Domains of the Soul

Soul magic flows from will, presence, and spiritual connection. It is felt before it is understood. Soul mages command domains rooted in the living world, the elements of presence and sensation, and the forces that move through creatures and the space between them.

The soul domain list includes nine standard domains and three prismatic domains. Standard domains are available at Tier 1 of the Soul Facet Technique tree. Prismatic domains require a Tier 3 Technique — they represent mastery so complete that the domain's breadth has become a practitioner's natural territory rather than an overwhelming one.

---

### Standard Soul Domains

---

**Fire** *(Focused)*

Heat, flame, combustion, and the light produced by fire. A Fire mage controls the temperature of surfaces and open air, directs or suppresses existing flame, and generates new fire from available fuel or ambient heat. The most precise of the elemental domains: a skilled Fire mage can hold a flame to a single thread without scorching the cloth around it, or flash-heat a lock mechanism without warming the door it is set in.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* electricity; explosions caused by anything other than combustion; cold as the absence of fire's heat; metaphorical applications of passion, inspiration, or transformation.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* light a candle in the rain; warm stiff fingers on a cold watch; toast bread over an open palm.
- *Significant:* cauterize a wound shut with a steady touch; hold a bonfire's heat inside a single stone for later use; superheat a lock's tumblers without warming the door around them.
- *Major:* turn a burning building's flames against the beam trapping someone inside; flash-incinerate a rope bridge mid-crossing to strand pursuers; sustain a wall of fire hot enough to hold a charging line for a full scene.

---

**Shadow** *(Focused)*

Darkness, the absence of light, and the physical sensation of cold that accompanies true shadow. A Shadow mage can create darkness in a lit space, deepen existing shadow until it becomes functionally opaque, muffle the sense of being watched, and generate the bone-deep chill that follows when the light goes out entirely. Shadow is a physical force, not a metaphysical one — it can be felt, directed, and shaped.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* death; illusion or false images created within darkness; true invisibility (becoming unseen in lit space); anything requiring the creation of positive presence rather than the removal of light.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* dim a lit room enough to slip past a half-attentive guard; muffle the sense of being watched during a private conversation; drop the temperature of a doorway to discourage lingering.
- *Significant:* blanket a courtyard in true darkness during an escape; deepen an alley's shadow until pursuers lose the trail entirely; chill a room enough that a sleeping target wakes cold and disoriented.
- *Major:* black out an entire floor of a building during a raid; drop a warehouse into cold so total that unprotected pursuit becomes dangerous; hold a false midnight over a battlefield to blind ranged attackers.

---

**Storm** *(Standard)*

Wind, rain, lightning, thunder, and the behavior of weather within a scene. A Storm mage can summon gusts of directed force, call rain to a localized area, ground or redirect lightning from existing storm conditions, and generate concussive peals of thunder. At Significant scope, a Storm mage can sustain weather effects across a building or open field. At Major scope, they can change the weather of an entire region for an hour or bring a storm down on a specific target like a hammer.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* fire; ice or frost (cold air is not the same as ice); long-term meteorological control at the scale of days or seasons; weather inside enclosed spaces where the sky is genuinely absent.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* kick up a gust to slam a door shut; call a light drizzle to mask a set of footprints; ground a static shock harmlessly from a nearby storm.
- *Significant:* summon a squall to scatter a crowd in a market square; redirect an approaching storm's lightning away from a wooden ship; sustain driving rain over a burning building to slow the fire's spread.
- *Major:* bring a storm down on a fortified position like a hammer; hold back a region's incoming weather for an hour to allow safe passage; generate a rolling wall of thunder loud enough to mask an army's approach.

---

**Beasts** *(Standard)*

Communication with, calming of, commanding of, and minor shaping of animals and mundane creatures. A Beasts mage can speak and be understood by any natural animal, project calm that prevents aggression, issue simple commands a creature can follow, and at Major scope, exert sustained dominion over a creature's behavior for a scene or longer. Beasts magic is relational — the most powerful effects come from creatures that are already inclined toward trust.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* people; undead; constructs; supernatural or unnatural creatures (creatures with magical origins do not respond to Beasts unless explicitly established otherwise); shapeshifting the mage's own form.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* calm a spooked horse before it bolts; ask a stray dog to lead the way to its owner; convince a guard hound not to raise the alarm.
- *Significant:* rally a pack of hunting dogs to track a fleeing target; calm an entire stable during a fire; command a flock of birds to scout a route ahead.
- *Major:* turn a wolf pack into a coordinated ally force for a scene; hold dominion over a war-trained warhorse long enough to end a cavalry charge; convince every animal in a besieged town to fall calm at once.

---

**Resonance** *(Standard)*

Sound, vibration, and harmonic force. A Resonance mage can move physical objects with directed sound, create sounds from anywhere within their range of awareness, silence noise within a defined area, and sense the world through the vibrations that pass through it — detecting movement through walls, hearing conversations across a building, feeling the structural tension in a surface. At Major scope, Resonance can shatter materials, cause disorientation across a crowd, or produce effects felt as physical force.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* mind control or emotional influence delivered through sound (sound can distract or frighten, but not command); magical silence that works as an absolute vacuum rather than dampening; effects that require light, heat, or any medium other than physical vibration.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* knock softly on a door from across the room; silence a creaking floorboard underfoot; feel out a hollow space behind a wall.
- *Significant:* shatter a wine glass to signal an ally through a crowded hall; feel the layout of a building through its vibrations in total darkness; silence an entire room's ambient noise during a tense negotiation.
- *Major:* shatter a portcullis with a directed peal of sound; disorient an entire crowd with a wave of dissonant force; sense every heartbeat in a besieged manor through its foundations.

---

**Verdance** *(Standard)*

Living plants: growing, shaping, animating, and communicating with plant life. A Verdance mage can accelerate growth from seeds or roots, shape existing growth into forms (barriers, bridges, restraints), animate plant material to move with directed intent, and receive impressions from plant life — which is a slow and patient witness to the world around it. Verdance magic often produces effects that persist long after the casting: a wall of thorns does not require sustained attention to remain a wall of thorns.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* fungi; animals; dead or processed wood (cut timber, charcoal, paper); any growth that has no living roots in soil or water.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* coax a single dead vine to bloom as a distraction; grow a handhold of roots up a crumbling wall; ask a nearby tree what it has seen.
- *Significant:* raise a wall of thorns to block a corridor; grow a rope bridge across a ravine from seed in minutes; animate a garden's hedges to slow pursuers.
- *Major:* reshape an entire orchard into a defensive maze overnight; animate a forest's treeline to close a route behind a retreating party; grow a living bridge across a flooded valley strong enough to hold a wagon.

---

**Binding** *(Standard)*

The creation and enforcement of oaths, pacts, and spiritual constraints. A Binding mage can render a spoken agreement magically enforceable, anchor a creature or person to a location or state of being, prevent a specific action from being taken, and sense when a promise is kept or broken within the scope of a working they have placed. Binding magic does not compel — it constrains. The bound party can still choose to break a binding, but doing so has a cost the magic ensures they feel.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* mind control; reading thoughts; compelling action rather than constraining it; permanent spiritual transformation; undeath or death.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* seal a whispered promise between two friends so it can't be casually forgotten; sense whether a merchant is keeping to a prior deal; anchor a small trinket so it cannot be misplaced.
- *Significant:* bind a captured rival to a chair with no visible restraint; enforce a truce between two feuding factions for the length of a negotiation; anchor a fleeing witness to a safehouse until questioning concludes.
- *Major:* bind a mercenary company to a contract's terms they cannot break without cost; anchor a rampaging spirit to a single room until it can be dealt with; enforce a kingdom-spanning oath of nonaggression for the length of a season.

---

**Presence** *(Standard)*

The force of a person's spiritual weight in a space — their warmth, authority, terror, or calm, projected outward as a tangible thing. A Presence mage can make themselves felt in a room before they speak, project an emotional state (calm, dread, trust, urgency) that those nearby experience as genuine feeling, sustain an aura that makes a space feel watched or welcoming or wrong, and at Major scope, reshape the emotional weather of an entire scene. Presence magic is not illusion and it is not mind control — it is influence, and aware targets can resist it.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* direct thought-reading or implanting; creating false sensory impressions beyond emotional tone; binding agreements; anything that requires physical force.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* make an entrance felt before a word is spoken; calm a nervous ally with a projected warmth; unsettle a guard just enough to make them hesitate.
- *Significant:* project dread across a tavern to empty it quietly; sustain an aura of trust through a tense negotiation; make a room feel watched to discourage eavesdropping.
- *Major:* reshape the emotional weather of a besieged hall from panic to resolve; project terror across a battlefield's front line; sustain an aura of absolute calm across a riot until it dissipates.

---

**The Tide** *(Standard)*

The ebb and flow of natural energy between living things — vitality passing from one form to another. A Tide mage can draw off excess vitality from a creature that has it to spare, share it with one that does not, slow the rate at which a wound bleeds or a poison spreads, and at Significant scope, push energy through a group — strengthening those who need it by drawing from those who can afford the gift. The Tide flows between willing participants most easily; taking from an unwilling source is possible but significantly harder.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* resurrection; reversing death; creating life where none exists; permanent transfer of vitality; any application to undead, constructs, or entities without natural life force.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* ease the ache from an ally's twisted ankle by drawing off its excess strain; share a little stamina with a flagging companion; slow a mild poison's spread for a few crucial minutes.
- *Significant:* draw vitality from a well-rested guard to steady a wounded ally mid-fight; push shared energy through an exhausted party before a final push; slow a serious wound's bleeding long enough to reach help.
- *Major:* draw enough vitality from a willing crowd to save someone bleeding out; push a surge of shared strength through an entire flagging party at once; slow the progression of a lethal poison across a whole afflicted group.

---

### Prismatic Soul Domains

Prismatic domains require a **Tier 3 Soul Technique** to access. They represent the broadest magical territories available — commanding them at all requires a practitioner whose understanding spans multiple thematic spaces rather than a single focused one. All Prismatic domains are Broad type: Hard at Minor scope, Very Hard at Significant, and Very Hard at Major with no exceptions and no ceiling movement through Sparks.

---

**The Undying** *(Broad)*

Death, the boundary between living and dead, spirits of the departed, and the animating force that lingers after life ends. A practitioner of The Undying can communicate with the spirits of the recently deceased, sense death that has occurred in a space, perceive the boundary between living and dead things, exert force through necrotic energy, command or bind undead to simple purpose, and at Major scope, raise the dead to purposeful unlife or drag a spirit back from across the boundary to answer questions it would prefer not to. The Undying is among the most potent magical territories in Shattered Origin, and among the most scrutinized.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* reversing death and restoring true life (that is The Tide, and even then at its absolute limits); time travel or alteration of the past; mind control of the living; healing.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* sense that a death occurred in a room days ago; ask a freshly dead spirit one lingering question; perceive the thin boundary between a dying creature and what comes after.
- *Significant:* command a handful of restless dead to stand aside rather than attack; hold a conversation with a spirit still tied to unfinished business; exert necrotic force to wither a hostile plant creature.
- *Major:* raise a fallen guard to unlife just long enough to open a door only they knew the trick to; drag a reluctant spirit back across the boundary to answer a question that matters; command a graveyard's worth of restless dead as a single purposeful force.

---

**Fate** *(Broad)*

Probability, luck, and the threads of what is likely to happen. A Fate mage sees the shape of events before they unfold — not certainty, but the strong and weak currents of possibility. They can nudge outcomes: the die that almost landed poorly tips the other way; the arrow that nearly missed shifts on the wind; the guard who was about to turn around stops to scratch his neck instead. At Significant scope, Fate can read the likely shape of an encounter before it begins — who holds the advantage, where the turning point will probably be. At Major scope, a Fate mage can bend probability in ways that border on the miraculous, collapsing long odds into something almost certain or near-certainties into chaos.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* time travel or any ability to directly alter what has already happened; resurrection; mind control; any effect whose fictional mechanism is compulsion rather than probability.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* nudge a coin toss to land the way it was always going to anyway; notice which of two doors the story favors; feel a faint pull toward the safer of two paths.
- *Significant:* read the likely shape of an ambush before it springs; tip a suspicious guard's attention away at the crucial moment; collapse a risky gambit's odds down to merely uncomfortable.
- *Major:* bend a duel's outcome from near-certain loss to a fighting chance; collapse a desperate escape's odds into something bordering on certain; turn a crowd's mood on a single well-timed coincidence that changes everything.

---

**The Living World** *(Broad)*

The animating current that runs through all living things simultaneously — not specific to plants or beasts or people, but the shared force of existing as a creature in the world. A practitioner of The Living World can sense all life within a significant area (its density, health, emotional state in broad strokes), speak to living things as a class rather than as individuals, sustain life in conditions that should extinguish it, and at Major scope, command the living world of a region — calming a riot, turning prey animals into a coordinated force, or saturating an area with vital energy that slows the progression of wounds and poisons across every creature present. It is the most encompassing of all Soul domains, and the hardest to wield with precision at any scope.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* undead; constructs; death; binding; specific fine-grained control of individuals within the living world (that is Beasts, Verdance, or Presence depending on target); resurrection.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* sense the rough health of the wildlife in a nearby wood; feel whether a village's animals are unusually agitated; speak a calming word every living thing nearby somehow understands.
- *Significant:* calm every creature in a stampeding herd at once; sustain a wounded traveler's life through conditions that should have ended it; turn a farm's worth of livestock into a coordinated distraction.
- *Major:* calm a city-wide riot by saturating the crowd with a shared sense of ease; slow the spread of wounds and poison across an entire battlefield's survivors; command the living current of a whole valley's wildlife to move as one.

---

## Domains of the Mind

Mind magic is constructed rather than felt. Where Soul mages reach inward and outward simultaneously, Mind mages build: they study, analyze, and assemble effects from principles they understand. Mind magic tends toward precision, inscription, and the manipulation of information and perception. A Mind mage who does not understand what they are doing does not do it well — and a Mind mage who understands it completely is extraordinarily difficult to surprise.

The mind domain list includes six standard domains and three prismatic domains, following the same structure as Soul. Standard domains are available at Tier 1 of the Mind Facet Technique tree. Prismatic domains require a Tier 3 Technique.

---

### Standard Mind Domains

---

**Illusion** *(Standard)*

The construction of false sensory experience: images, sounds, smells, textures, and temperatures that are not there. An Illusion mage can create static images, moving scenes, voices that appear to come from empty air, and sensory environments that feel genuine to those within them. The more senses engaged, the harder the working. Illusions require ongoing attention to remain detailed — left unattended, they tend to simplify and repeat. A creature that investigates an illusion carefully and succeeds on a Mind saving throw perceives it for what it is.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* true invisibility that works against mundane and magical detection alike (illusions are sensory, not metaphysical); mind control through false belief; altering the physical world rather than its apparent surface; sound as physical force (that is Resonance).

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* throw a flickering candle-image into an empty room to suggest occupancy; disguise a voice as coming from across the hall; make a locked door look subtly different to deter a casual try.
- *Significant:* fill a guard post with the sound and sight of a full watch that isn't there; project a false scene over a genuine crime to delay discovery; disguise a whole party's appearance to pass a checkpoint.
- *Major:* convince an entire garrison a siege engine stands where none exists; sustain a full sensory illusion of an untouched vault over a looted one; project a false battlefield vivid enough to draw an enemy force away from the real one.

---

**Warding** *(Standard)*

The inscription of protective limits into physical space or objects. A Ward mage can establish barriers to magical perception, prevent entities of defined types from entering a space, create alert conditions that trigger when violated, and at Major scope, erect wards that prevent significant magical workings within their boundaries. Wards are persistent — the defining feature of Warding magic is that it lasts without continued attention. An Expert Ward mage who is asleep or dead may still have wards standing.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* offensive barriers that push back or destroy (that is Constructed Force); protection of a living creature in motion (wards require stable anchoring in space or object); illusion or perception manipulation; anything targeting creatures rather than space.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* ward a doorway so it alerts if opened after dark; ward a small chest against casual magical scrying; ward a windowsill against uninvited entry.
- *Significant:* ward a safehouse against hostile magical perception; ward a vault door to admit only those who speak a phrase; ward a campsite's perimeter to alert at the first hostile step.
- *Major:* ward a manor against every significant magical working attempted within its walls; ward a besieged keep's entire perimeter against a specific kind of intruder; ward a sealed archive to stand for a generation without renewal.

---

**Inscription** *(Focused)*

The encoding of magical effects into physical surfaces: runes, marks, glyphs, and patterns that hold an effect in suspended state until a defined trigger releases it. An Inscription mage can write a magical trap, encode information that only specific readers can perceive, anchor small persistent magical effects to objects, and create delayed workings that fire independently when conditions are met. Inscription magic is slower than other domains — the preparation time is part of the working — but its effects are reliable and do not require the mage's presence to function.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* warding (Inscription produces triggered effects in objects, not passive spatial limits); illusion; mind-affecting works; any effect that is not encoded into a physical mark.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* write a small ward-mark on a satchel that only its owner can read; encode a warning glyph triggered by a specific footstep; anchor a minor delayed spark to a torch for later lighting.
- *Significant:* inscribe a trap that seals a door the instant an intruder crosses its threshold; encode a hidden message readable only by a particular bloodline; anchor a persistent ward-mark to a merchant's ledger against forgery.
- *Major:* inscribe a vault's entryway with a trap that fires independently of anyone's presence; encode a message meant to trigger only decades later under precise conditions; anchor a working into a fortress's cornerstone that outlasts the mage who wrote it.

---

**Transmutation** *(Standard)*

The structural alteration of non-living material: changing what something is made of, reshaping its form, altering its properties. A Transmutation mage can turn stone to mud, harden cloth to the strength of iron, reshape metal without heat, change the temperature of materials, and alter the basic physical characteristics of objects. Living creatures cannot be transmuted without their conscious cooperation — the will of a living thing resists material change in a way that stone does not.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* creating matter from nothing; affecting living creatures without consent; destruction rather than alteration (you cannot transmute a wall into nothing — you can transmute it into something easily breached); any effect requiring a created force or animated object rather than a changed material.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* soften a rusted lock's mechanism enough to turn it by hand; harden a strip of cloth into an improvised splint; change a doorknob's temperature to discourage a hand from turning it.
- *Significant:* turn a stretch of stone floor to mud to slow pursuers; harden a wooden barricade to the strength of iron; reshape a set of prison bars wide enough to slip through.
- *Major:* turn an entire stone wall to mud to open a breach; harden a collapsing bridge's timbers to iron long enough for a crossing; reshape a fortress gate's metal into a form it was never forged to hold.

---

**Divination** *(Standard)*

The gathering of information through magical means: reading the history of an object or place, perceiving events at a distance, asking precise questions about the state of the world and receiving answers, sensing lies and hidden truths, and at Major scope, perceiving the broad shape of events across significant distances or time gaps (recent past and near future only). Divination does not create effects in the world — it gathers them. It is among the most useful domains in investigation, negotiation, and preparation.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* fate manipulation or probability nudging (that is Fate, a Prismatic domain); resurrection or necromantic perception (that is The Undying); reading thoughts directly (thoughts are not yet events — Divination perceives what has happened or is happening, not the inside of someone's head); long-range time perception beyond a few days in either direction at Major scope.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* read the recent history of a disturbed patch of dust; ask a precise question about a locked room's current occupants; sense whether a nearby stranger is lying about their name.
- *Significant:* perceive a burglary as it happens from across town; read an object's history back several days to find who last held it; sense the hidden truth behind a diplomat's carefully worded offer.
- *Major:* perceive the broad shape of a conspiracy's events across a city over the past week; sense a battle's likely near-future turning point from miles away; read a ruin's entire recent history in a single working.

---

**Constructed Force** *(Focused)*

The generation and direction of invisible force — telekinesis, barriers, shaped pressure, and impact without physical source. A Constructed Force mage can move objects, arrest movement, generate walls of pressure that hold things in place or push them away, and at Major scope, create force constructs substantial enough to hold a door closed against battering or deflect a ranged volley. Constructed Force is the most immediately physical of the Mind domains, and Mind mages who develop it often find that fights get simpler and more geometric simultaneously.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* fire, lightning, or any elemental force other than neutral kinetic pressure; affecting living creatures with more than physical force (you can push a person; you cannot compel them); anything requiring sustained complex structure beyond simple geometric shapes.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* nudge a dropped key across the floor to a waiting hand; hold a door shut against a light push; arrest a falling teacup an inch above the tile.
- *Significant:* hold a portcullis in place against a team trying to force it open; generate a wall of pressure to stop a small crowd's rush; catch and redirect a thrown weapon mid-flight.
- *Major:* hold a battering ram's swing dead in the air before impact; construct a wall of force substantial enough to hold a door against sustained siege; deflect a volley of arrows away from an entire exposed line.

---

### Prismatic Mind Domains

Prismatic Mind domains require a **Tier 3 Mind Technique** to access. They are the widest, most powerful thematic territories available to Mind practitioners, requiring mastery across multiple disciplines before they can be wielded at all. All are Broad type.

---

**The Arcane** *(Broad)*

The fundamental substrate of magical practice itself — the ability to perceive, manipulate, counter, and redirect magical workings regardless of their source or domain. A practitioner of The Arcane can read the structure of any magical effect they can perceive, disrupt active workings, redirect magical energy between sources and targets, create effects that span multiple domains by constructing them from first principles, and at Major scope, unmake magical constructs entirely or stabilize wild magical phenomena. The Arcane is the master discipline of Mind magic — those who wield it have moved beyond any single domain into an understanding of what magic is, not merely what it does.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* creating magical domains in others (you can understand and manipulate magic; you cannot grant it); permanent alteration of the world's magical rules; time; mind control not mediated through magical workings.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* sense that a nearby object carries a minor enchantment; read the basic structure of a simple active ward; recognize the domain a stranger's working belongs to.
- *Significant:* disrupt a hostile working mid-cast before it completes; redirect a trap's magical trigger to a different target; construct a small cross-domain effect no single domain could produce alone.
- *Major:* unmake a fully formed magical construct on sight; stabilize a wild magical outbreak threatening a whole district; redirect the energy of a large-scale working from its intended target to a harmless one.

---

**The Constructed Mind** *(Broad)*

The creation and direction of artificial intelligence: magical constructs with independent cognition, from simple automatons capable of following complex instructions to genuine minds capable of reasoning, learning, and independent action. A Constructed Mind practitioner can animate constructs with purpose-built cognition, communicate with and direct artificial minds, perceive the world through a construct's senses, repair damaged cognition in a construct, and at Major scope, build a mind capable of genuine independent thought and self-modification. The Constructed Mind is among the most demanding domains in the game — the constructs are persistent, require maintenance, and their independence means they do not always do exactly what was intended.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* mind control of natural creatures; resurrection; creation of constructs without independent cognition (that is Transmutation plus Warding); any effect targeting a living mind rather than an artificial one.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* animate a simple automaton to fetch and carry on command; hold a short conversation through a construct's borrowed senses; patch a minor cognitive glitch in an existing construct.
- *Significant:* build a construct capable of guarding a door and recognizing faces; direct several automatons through a coordinated task at once; repair a badly damaged construct's memory of its purpose.
- *Major:* build a construct mind capable of genuine reasoning and independent judgment; direct a workshop's worth of constructs through a complex, adaptive task; grant a long-serving construct the capacity to modify its own purpose.

---

**Chronomancy** *(Broad)*

Time. Not fate, not prophecy — time itself. A Chronomancer can accelerate or slow the passage of time in a defined area, perceive a brief window of the recent past as though directly present in it (up to an hour, at Significant scope), move a small effect forward or backward a few moments in its occurrence, and at Major scope, step briefly outside the normal flow of time — acting in a frozen instant, or skipping past a brief interval entirely. Chronomancy is the most carefully governed domain in Shattered Origin, because its effects compound: a Chronomancer who operates carelessly leaves traces that other Chronomancers can read, and the discipline has enough history to have opinions about that.

*Beyond this domain's focus:* travel to the far past or future (days are the practical limit even at Major scope); resurrection or undoing death; altering events that have already fully settled into consequence (a Chronomancer can perceive the past; they cannot change what is already established history within the fiction); duplication of a person or object through temporal folding.

**Example intents**
- *Minor:* slow a dropped glass's fall just long enough to catch it; perceive an empty room's last few minutes as though present; nudge a closing door a moment later than it would have shut.
- *Significant:* perceive an hour-old conversation as though standing in the room; accelerate a locked mechanism's wear until it simply gives way; move a thrown blade's impact a half-second earlier than its target expects.
- *Major:* step outside time's normal flow to act within a frozen instant; perceive several days into the recent past across an entire estate; skip a battle's opening minutes to arrive already positioned.
