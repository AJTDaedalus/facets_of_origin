# Scenario: The Ashwood Trail

## Purpose (PT04 — Resource Tax Session)

Test multiple sequential encounters without full recovery between fights. This validates the "Three Encounter Session" template and answers: does Endurance carry-over create meaningful tension across a session?

### What We're Testing

1. **Endurance carry-over** — Does partial Endurance entering a fight create genuine tension?
2. **Spark pacing** — Do Sparks run out at the right time (during the Hard fight, not before)?
3. **Skirmish as warm-up** — Does encounter 1 feel like a confidence-builder or a resource tax?
4. **Hard fight survivability after prior encounters** — Is the climax still winnable?
5. **Session arc feel** — Does rising tension from fight to fight create a satisfying session?
6. **Spark Cadence** — Test the structured earning methods (Act Break Nomination between encounters)

### Key Metrics to Track

- Endurance at start of each encounter (per PC)
- Sparks at start of each encounter (per PC)
- Sparks spent per encounter
- Sparks earned via Nomination Rounds between encounters
- Win/loss per encounter
- PCs Broken per encounter
- Total exchanges per encounter
- Player sentiment per encounter (quotes about tension/fun)

## MM's Prep Notes

### Campaign Pitch (One Paragraph)

The party is escorting a merchant caravan through the Ashwood — a dense forest known for bandit activity. Three days of travel, three encounters of escalating danger. The merchant, Tella, is paying well but keeping secrets about what's in her carts. The party doesn't need to know (yet). The journey is the session.

### Session Structure (Three Encounter Template)

**Encounter 1 — Skirmish: The Scout Party (15 min)**
*Narrative:* Day one, late afternoon. Three bandits watching the trail from a ridge. They see the party, nock arrows, and charge. Overconfident, poorly equipped.
*Difficulty:* Skirmish — the party should win cleanly.
*Purpose:* Warm-up. Introduce combat, let players feel competent. Tax a small amount of Endurance.

**Nomination Round 1 (5 min between encounters)**
After the skirmish, call for an Act Break Nomination. Each player nominates one other player for something cool they did in the fight or the approach to it.

**Encounter 2 — Standard: The Bridge Ambush (30 min)**
*Narrative:* Day two, midday. The trail crosses a narrow stone bridge over a gorge. Halfway across, bandits emerge from both sides — they've been waiting. The bridge limits movement (narrative constraint, no special mechanic).
*Difficulty:* Standard — a real fight. Someone should take conditions.
*Purpose:* The real test. Players should feel the Endurance they lost in encounter 1.

**Nomination Round 2 (5 min between encounters)**
After the ambush, call for nominations again. Also test: does the MM see a natural moment for a Graceful Failure Spark if anyone rolled 6-?

**Encounter 3 — Hard: The Bandit Captain (45 min)**
*Narrative:* Day three, evening. The bandit captain and her elite squad intercept the caravan at camp. She wants what's in the carts. She's willing to kill for it. The party is defending Tella and the caravan.
*Difficulty:* Hard — designed as a coin flip. Someone may get Broken.
*Purpose:* The climax. Can the party win after two prior fights with depleted Endurance?

---

## Recovery Between Encounters

**Between Encounter 1 and 2:**
- Short narrative rest (making camp, eating, watch shifts)
- Endurance recovery: each PC may spend 1 exchange equivalent in Withdrawn posture = recover 2 Endurance
- Conditions: all Tier 1 cleared (they clear end of exchange anyway). Tier 2 persists unless treated.
- Sparks: session Sparks persist. Earned Sparks from Nomination Round add to pool.

**Between Encounter 2 and 3:**
- Same recovery rules
- The Hard fight should start with PCs at 60-80% Endurance if previous fights went Standard difficulty

## Enemies

### Encounter 1: Bandit Scouts (Skirmish)

**Bandit Scout (Mook)** — TR 1
- Attack modifier: +0
- No Endurance (Mook)
- No armor
- Tactics: Charge in, target whoever looks weakest
- 3 scouts total (PS 3 party) or 4 scouts (PS 4 party)

**Encounter Budget:** PS 3 × 1 (Skirmish) = 3. Raw TR 3 (3 Mooks × 1). Correct.

### Encounter 2: Bridge Bandits (Standard)

**Bandit Warrior (Mook)** — TR 1
- Attack modifier: +0
- No Endurance (Mook)
- No armor
- 4 warriors total

**Bandit Archer (Mook)** — TR 1
- Attack modifier: +0
- No Endurance (Mook)
- No armor
- 2 archers (positioned at range — narrative flavor, same mechanics)

**Encounter Budget:** PS 3 × 2 (Standard) = 6. Raw TR 6 (6 Mooks × 1). With tier-weighted formula: 6 × 0.5 × 1.1 (6 enemies) = 3.3 effective TR. Skirmish-leaning by new math — may need adjustment. Consider replacing 2 Mooks with 1 Named if this plays too easy.

**Variant (if too easy):** Replace 4 Mook Warriors with 1 Named Bandit Lieutenant:
- **Bandit Lieutenant (Named)** — TR 8
  - Endurance: 4
  - Attack modifier: +1
  - Defense modifier: +0
  - No armor
  - Use with 2 Mook Archers. Budget check: (8 × 1.0 + 2 × 1 × 0.5) × 1.0 = 9.0 effective TR. Standard.

### Encounter 3: Bandit Captain (Hard)

**Bandit Captain (Named NPC)** — TR 9
- Endurance: 5
- Attack modifier: +1
- Defense modifier: +1
- Armor: Light
- Technique: Rally — once per exchange, one Mook that was removed returns (represents reinforcements)
- TR: Offense 3 + Durability 3 + Armor 1 + Technique 1 + minimum Named TR applied = **9**
- Posture: Measured
- Personality: Professional. Offers surrender first. "Hand over the cargo. Nobody has to die tonight."

**Elite Bandit (Mook)** — TR 1
- Attack modifier: +0
- No Endurance (Mook)
- No armor
- 3 elite bandits with the Captain

**Encounter Budget:** PS 3 × 3 (Hard) = 9. Tier-weighted: (9 × 1.0 + 3 × 1 × 0.5) × 1.1 = (9 + 1.5) × 1.1 = 11.55 effective TR. Above Hard budget — good, since the party should be depleted.

**Lateral Solution (available but not highlighted):**
- If a player asks about the cargo, Tella reveals it's a shipment of ward-stones — magical components. A PC with a magic domain could activate one as a defensive barrier (Knowledge or Spirit check, Standard), giving the party advantage for one exchange.
- The Captain will accept negotiation — she actually wants the ward-stones, not blood. A Charisma check (Hard) could end the fight with a deal.
