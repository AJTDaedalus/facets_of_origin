## ACT I — THE APPROACH

### Scene 1: The Road to Ashenmoor

**Jordan (MM):** *You've been walking since midday. The road to Ashenmoor follows the river Mire — it's a mining village, three days from the nearest town, the kind of place that exists because someone found iron in the hills and people followed the iron. The magistrate's briefing was straightforward: Ashenmoor has gone silent. Not metaphorically. Literally. No letters. A merchant who passed through said the villagers moved normally but could not speak, could not sing, could not scream. You're being paid 80 silver and a writ of authority to find out why.*

*As you crest the last hill before the village, the first thing you notice is the absence. Not of people — you can see smoke from chimneys, movement in the fields. The absence of sound. No birdsong. No insect hum. The wind moves the grass, but it seems... quieter than it should be. Like the world has its finger on a volume dial and is slowly turning it down.*

**Morgan (Sable):** I stop walking and close my eyes. I want to feel the space — is this spiritual corruption, or something else? My Specialty says I can sense when something in a space has been spiritually altered.

**Jordan:** That's a great use of your Specialty. You don't need to roll for this — the Specialty gives you the read directly. Sable, standing at the top of the hill with her eyes closed and the wind in her hair: yes. This is wrong. Not just wrong — *structured*. The silence isn't random suppression. It's radiating from a point. Like ripples in reverse, but in sound. Something at the center of this village — or beneath it — is pulling sound toward itself.

**Morgan:** "Something is drawing sound inward. Toward a center. This isn't natural."

**Taylor (Rowan):** "Toward the mine?"

**Morgan:** "I don't know yet. But there's a structure to it. Whatever this is, it was built. Not born."

> **Casey (Lyra):** I pull up my character sheet in the Tools tab. My Specialty is "can identify magical constructs, wards, and inscription patterns on sight." Can I see anything from up here?

**Jordan:** Not from this distance — you'd need to be closer to see inscription patterns. But note that instinct. It'll matter later.

### Scene 2: Entering the Village

**Jordan (MM):** *You walk into Ashenmoor proper. The village is small — thirty buildings, maybe. A market square with a well. A tavern called The Pickaxe with its sign creaking in the wind. A man hammers at a horseshoe at the smithy — you can see the hammer fall but the clang arrives late, softened. Children play in the square but they play in silence, their mouths moving in shapes that carry no sound.*

*A man approaches you. He's in his sixties, thin, wearing a chain of office that marks him as someone official. He holds up a slate on which he has written: "YOU ARE THE MAGISTRATE'S PEOPLE? I AM ALDERMAN HALE. FOLLOW ME."*

**Taylor:** Rowan sizes him up. "Alderman. What happened here?"

**Jordan (as Hale):** *He writes on the slate, chalk scratching audibly — that's surprising, you can hear the chalk — "IT STARTED THREE WEEKS AGO. MINE OPENING. THE SILENCE CAME OUT."*

**Casey:** Can I read anything about the nature of this from the inscription patterns in the village? Walking through, am I seeing anything magical?

**Casey:** "The silence is... feeding on where the voices should be. It gets deeper where there are more people."

## ACT II — THE MINE

**Jordan (MM):** *The mine entrance is a square timber-framed opening in the hillside, dark as a throat. Your torches push the darkness back about fifteen feet. The air is cold and still — mine cold, the kind that doesn't change with seasons. Your footsteps echo, but the echoes come back wrong. Shorter than they should be. As if the tunnels are absorbing the sound rather than reflecting it.*

*The upper tunnels are standard mining infrastructure: timber supports, iron rail tracks for ore carts, occasional alcoves with rusted equipment. A wheelbarrow filled with ore samples sits where someone left it years ago. The map shows two routes: a main shaft descending via a series of switchbacks, and a narrower service tunnel.*

**Taylor:** I want to check the main shaft for structural integrity before we go down. I was City Watch, not a miner, but I know what a dangerous building looks like.

**Jordan:** That's Wisdom — reading the environment. Standard difficulty. Your Watch background doesn't directly apply to mines, but reading whether a structure is safe is a transferable skill.

> **[Roll #4] 2d6(5,5) + Wisdom(+0) + Standard(+0) = 10 → Full Success**

**Jordan:** Ten. Full success. Rowan inspects the supports, tests a timber with the pommel of his sword, checks the rail tracks for warping. His assessment: the main shaft is solid. These tunnels were built to last — quality timber, good engineering, well-maintained before they were sealed. The service tunnel, though, has water damage near its entrance. If you go that way, there may be collapses.

**Taylor:** "Main shaft. Stay together, watch the walls, standard two-and-two formation. Drew and I in front, Casey and Morgan behind us."

**Drew:** "What's two-and-two formation?"

*The party descends into the mine in formation.*

### Scene 6: The Silence Deepens

**Jordan (MM):** *As you descend through the switchbacks, the quality of sound changes. Your footsteps, which echoed in the upper tunnels, start to sound flat. Muted. Like walking on carpet instead of stone, but the stone is right there under your boots. Casey, your breathing sounds louder to you because everything else is getting quieter.*

*You reach the middle tunnels. The map shows this level had active mining operations — there are branching passages, tool storage rooms, and a drinking-water cistern marked on the map. The rail tracks continue down.*

**Casey:** I'm going to check for magical traces as we walk. Not a roll — just keeping my eyes open for inscription patterns, wards, anything constructed.

**Jordan:** Good. You don't see formal inscriptions, but you notice something. The natural mineral veins in the rock walls — iron ore, mostly — have a faint crystalline shimmer that isn't quite natural. It's like the mineral deposits have been resonating for so long that they've partially crystallized. It's beautiful, actually. Thin lines of crystal threading through the ore like veins of glass.

**Casey:** "The minerals are crystallizing. Whatever is down below has been operating long enough to change the geology."

**Morgan:** "How long?"

**Casey:** "Years? Decades? This isn't three weeks of silence. This is something that's been growing."

**Jordan:** Hale said the mine was sealed by his *grandfather*. That's at minimum forty years ago. Whatever is down there has been building power for a long time. The three weeks of silence is just when it reached the surface.

**Taylor:** "Faster we move, better. But carefully — this isn't the kind of place where you run."

**Jordan (MM):** *You round a corner in the middle tunnels and Rowan's torch illuminates something ahead. A side tunnel — not on the map — has been... opened. ## ACT III — THE HEART

### Scene 9: The Resonance Chamber (Boss Encounter — TR 14, Deadly)

**Jordan (MM):** *The lower tunnels open into something that was never mined. A natural cavern, vast, cathedral-high. Stalactites hang like pipe organ tubes from the ceiling. The walls are covered — floor to ceiling — in the same crystalline formations you've seen threading through the rock, but here they're thick. Clusters of crystal the size of a person's arm, humming with a vibration you feel in your teeth rather than hear.*

*And at the center of the cavern: The Resonance.*

*A crystalline formation the size of a person, growing from the cavern floor like a tree made of glass. It pulses with light — but the light follows no rhythm you recognize. Then you realize: the rhythm is words. Thousands of stolen voices, compressed into light, playing too fast and too dense to distinguish. Every voice the village has lost is in there. Every conversation, every song, every scream that never happened. All of it. Compressed into a crystal that pulses like a heartbeat.*

*It is not alive. It is a device. And it is doing exactly what it was designed to do.*

**Casey:** "It's a construct. Someone built this." I pull out Maren's frequency map drawing. "Morgan — can you read the counter-frequency now? With the source right in front of you?"

**Morgan:** I study the crystal's vibration pattern and compare it to Maren's circles. This is the moment — Attune check to find the counter-frequency.

**Jordan:** This is the lateral solution. Attune + Spirit, Hard difficulty — but Maren's drawings make it one step easier, so Standard. If you succeed, you can shatter the crystal without a fight.

**Morgan:** I'm going to spend a Spark. This is the most important roll of the session.

**Casey:** "What happens if you sing three out of four?"

**Jordan:** The crystal activates its defense. It heard you reaching for the counter-frequency, and it responds.

*The Resonance pulses. A wave of stolen voices washes outward — thousands of whispers at once, overlapping, contradicting, drowning. The crystal knows you're here, and it is not going to let you sing it to death.*

**Jordan:** Boss encounter. The Resonance: TR 14. No enemies with it initially, but that will change. Boss tier — Tier 2 incoming on attacks, phase change when Staggered. **Casey:** "I hear my own breathing again. Normal breathing."

**Taylor:** Rowan puts a hand on Sable's shoulder. "It worked."
